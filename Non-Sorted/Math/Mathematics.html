Mathematics ​

Mathematics is the science and study of quantity, structure, space, and change. It is used to model and analyse real-world phenomena, as well as to solve problems in various fields such as science, engineering, and economics

Foundations ​

Mathematics is built on a foundation of axioms, definitions, and logical reasoning. Some key concepts in the foundations of mathematics include:

  1. Axioms: Basic assumptions or statements that are taken to be true without proof. Axioms serve as the starting point for mathematical reasoning and the development of mathematical theories

  2. Definitions: Precise descriptions of mathematical objects, concepts, and relationships. Definitions provide the language and framework for mathematical discourse

  3. Theorems: Statements that can be proven to be true based on axioms, definitions, and logical reasoning. Theorems are the building blocks of mathematical knowledge and are used to establish new results

  4. Proofs: Logical arguments that demonstrate the truth of a mathematical statement. Proofs are used to establish the validity of theorems and to derive new results from existing ones

  5. Logic: The study of reasoning and inference. Logic provides the rules and principles for constructing valid arguments and proofs in mathematics

  6. Set Theory: The study of sets, which are collections of objects. Set theory provides the foundation for modern mathematics and is used to define mathematical structures and relationships

  7. Mathematical Logic: The study of formal systems, proof theory, and model theory. Mathematical logic is used to study the structure and properties of mathematical reasoning

  8. Category Theory: The study of mathematical structures and relationships between them. Category theory provides a unified framework for understanding and comparing different areas of mathematics

  9. Theory of Computation: The study of algorithms, computability, and complexity. The theory of computation is used to analyse the capabilities and limitations of computational systems

    • Complexity theory: The study of the resources required to solve computational problems, such as time and space

Godel's incompleteness theorems show that there are limits to what can be proven within a formal mathematical system. These theorems demonstrate that there are true mathematical statements that cannot be proven using the axioms and rules of a given system

Pure Mathematics ​

Pure mathematics is the study of mathematical concepts independently of any application outside mathematics. It is concerned with the theoretical development of mathematical principles and structures

Number Systems ​

Numbers are the basic building blocks of mathematics. They can be classified into different types, such as:

  1. Natural Numbers: The set of positive integers 1, 2, 3, ...
  2. Integers: The set of whole numbers and their negatives ..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...
  3. Rational Numbers: Numbers that can be expressed as a ratio of two integers p/q, where q ≠ 0 (e.g., 1/2, 3/4)
  4. Irrational Numbers: Numbers that cannot be expressed as a ratio of two integers and have non-repeating, non-terminating decimal expansions (e.g., π, √2)
  5. Real Numbers: Numbers that can be represented on the number line, including rational and irrational numbers (e.g., π, √2)
  6. Complex Numbers: Numbers of the form a + bi, where a and b are real numbers and i is the imaginary unit (i^2 = -1)
  7. Quaternions: Numbers of the form a + bi + cj + dk, where a, b, c, and d are real numbers and i, j, and k are the imaginary units
  8. Cardinal Numbers: Numbers used to describe the size of sets (e.g., 0, 1, 2, 3, ...)
  9. Ordinal Numbers: Numbers used to describe the order or position of elements in a sequence (e.g., 1st, 2nd, 3rd, ...)
  10. Prime Numbers: Numbers greater than 1 that have no divisors other than 1 and themselves (e.g., 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, ...)

Infinity ​

Infinity is a concept in mathematics that represents a quantity without bound or end. It is used to describe values that are larger than any finite number or smaller than any positive number. There are different types of infinity, such as:

  1. Countable Infinity: The cardinality of a countably infinite set, such as the set of natural numbers N (e.g., 1, 2, 3, ...)
  2. Uncountable Infinity: The cardinality of an uncountably infinite set, such as the set of real numbers R (e.g., π, √2)

Some infinite series are larger than others. For example, there are infinitely many natural numbers, but there are more real numbers than natural numbers. This is known as the "infinity of infinities" problem

Structures ​

Mathematical structures are sets with operations defined on them that satisfy certain properties. Some common mathematical structures include:

  1. Groups: Sets with a binary operation that satisfies closure, associativity, identity, and invertibility properties (e.g., integers under addition)
  2. Rings: Sets with two binary operations (addition and multiplication) that satisfy closure, associativity, commutativity, distributivity, and identity properties (e.g., integers under addition and multiplication)
  3. Fields: Sets with two binary operations (addition and multiplication) that satisfy closure, associativity, commutativity, distributivity, identity, and invertibility properties (e.g., rational numbers)
  4. Vector Spaces: Sets of vectors with operations of addition and scalar multiplication that satisfy closure, associativity, commutativity, distributivity, identity, and invertibility properties (e.g., Euclidean space)
  5. Graphs: Structures consisting of vertices and edges that represent relationships between objects (e.g., social networks, transportation networks)
  6. Topological Spaces: Sets with a topology defined on them that captures the notion of continuity and convergence (e.g., Euclidean space with the Euclidean topology)
  7. Metric Spaces: Sets with a distance function (metric) defined on them that captures the notion of distance and convergence (e.g., Euclidean space with the Euclidean metric)

Number Theory ​

Number theory (the study of numbers) is a branch of mathematics that deals with the properties and relationships of numbers, especially integers. It involves the study of divisibility, prime numbers, congruences, Diophantine equations, and arithmetic functions

Algebra ​

Algebra (the study of formulas and related structures) is a branch of mathematics that deals with symbols and the rules for manipulating those symbols. It involves the study of mathematical structures such as groups, rings, fields, and vector spaces, as well as operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, and exponentiation

Linear Algebra ​

Linear algebra is a branch of algebra that deals with vector spaces and linear mappings between these spaces. It involves the study of vectors, matrices, determinants, systems of linear equations, eigenvalues, eigenvectors, and inner product spaces

  • Vectors
  • Matrices

Combinatorics ​

Combinatorics is a branch of mathematics that deals with counting, arranging, and selecting objects. It involves the study of permutations, combinations, graphs, trees, and networks

Set Theory ​

Set theory is a branch of mathematics that deals with sets, which are collections of objects. It involves the study of set operations, cardinality, ordinals, and transfinite numbers

Graph Theory ​

Graph theory is a branch of mathematics that deals with graphs, which are structures consisting of vertices and edges. It involves the study of connectivity, paths, cycles, trees, planarity, colouring, and matching

Order Theory ​

Order theory is a branch of mathematics that deals with partially ordered sets, which are sets with a binary relation that captures the notion of order. It involves the study of lattices, posets, chains, antichains, and order-preserving functions

Spaces ​

Mathematical spaces are sets with additional structure that allow for the study of geometric, topological, and algebraic properties. Some common mathematical spaces include:

  1. Euclidean Space: A space with a Cartesian coordinate system and the Euclidean metric that captures the notion of distance and angles (e.g., 2D plane, 3D space)
  2. Metric Space: A space with a distance function (metric) defined on it that captures the notion of distance and convergence (e.g., Euclidean space with the Euclidean metric)
  3. Topological Space: A space with a topology defined on it that captures the notion of continuity and convergence (e.g., Euclidean space with the Euclidean topology)
  4. Hilbert Space: A space with an inner product defined on it that captures the notion of angles and lengths (e.g., Euclidean space with the Euclidean inner product)
  5. Banach Space: A normed vector space that is complete with respect to the norm (e.g., Euclidean space with the Euclidean norm)
  6. Manifold: A space that locally resembles Euclidean space but may have a more complicated global structure (e.g., sphere, torus)
  7. Vector Space: A space of vectors with operations of addition and scalar multiplication that satisfy certain properties (e.g., Euclidean space)

Geometry ​

Geometry (the study of shapes and spaces that contain them) is a branch of mathematics that deals with the properties and relationships of points, lines, angles, surfaces, and solids. It involves the study of shapes, sizes, dimensions, and spatial relationships

Trigonometry ​

Trigonometry is a branch of mathematics that deals with the relationships between the sides and angles of triangles. It involves the study of trigonometric functions such as sine, cosine, tangent, secant, cosecant, and cotangent

Fractal Geometry ​

Fractal geometry is a branch of mathematics that deals with the study of fractals, which are complex geometric shapes that exhibit self-similarity at different scales. It involves the study of fractal dimensions, attractors, and self-replicating patterns

Topology ​

Topology is a branch of mathematics that deals with the study of spaces and their properties under continuous transformations. It involves the study of open sets, closed sets, continuity, compactness, connectedness, and convergence

Measure Theory ​

Measure theory is a branch of mathematics that deals with the study of measures, which are functions that assign a size to subsets of a space. It involves the study of measurable sets, measurable functions, integration, and probability

Differential Geometry ​

Differential geometry is a branch of mathematics that deals with the study of curves, surfaces, and manifolds using differential calculus and linear algebra. It involves the study of tangent spaces, curvature, geodesics, and Riemannian metrics

Changes ​

Change is a fundamental concept in mathematics that involves the study of rates of change, derivatives, differentials, integrals, and differential equations. Some common mathematical topics related to change include:

  1. Calculus: The mathematical study of change, including differential calculus and integral calculus
  2. Differential Equations: Equations that involve derivatives and describe the relationship between a function and its rate of change
  3. Partial Differential Equations: Differential equations that involve partial derivatives and describe the relationship between a function of multiple variables and its partial rates of change
  4. Integral Equations: Equations that involve integrals and describe the relationship between a function and its accumulated change
  5. Functional Analysis: The study of vector spaces with additional structure, such as norms and inner products, that allows for the analysis of functions and operators
  6. Dynamical Systems: Systems that evolve over time according to a set of rules or equations, such as differential equations or difference equations
  7. Chaos Theory: The study of complex and unpredictable systems that are highly sensitive to initial conditions, leading to chaotic behaviour
  8. Control Theory: The study of systems and processes that can be controlled or regulated using feedback mechanisms and control algorithms

Calculus ​

Calculus is the mathematical study of change. It has two main branches: differential calculus and integral calculus. Differential calculus deals with the concept of the derivative, while integral calculus deals with the concept of the integral

Vector Calculus ​

Vector calculus is a branch of calculus that deals with vector fields and vector-valued functions. It involves the study of vector operations, line integrals, surface integrals, and the theorems of Green, Gauss, and Stokes

Dynamical Systems ​

Dynamical systems are systems that evolve over time according to a set of rules or equations. They can be described using differential equations, difference equations, or other mathematical models. Dynamical systems can exhibit a wide range of behaviours, including stability, periodicity, chaos, and bifurcations

  • Fluid flow
  • Ecosystems

Chaos Theory ​

Chaos theory is the study of complex and unpredictable systems that are highly sensitive to initial conditions. It involves the study of deterministic systems that exhibit chaotic behaviour, such as sensitivity to initial conditions, bifurcations, strange attractors, and fractal structures

Complex Analysis ​

Complex analysis is a branch of mathematics that deals with complex numbers and functions. It involves the study of complex functions, contour integrals, analyticity, singularities, and the Cauchy-Riemann equations

Applied Mathematics ​

Applied mathematics is the study of mathematical methods that are used in science, engineering, business, computer science, and industry. It involves the application of mathematical techniques to solve real-world problems

Mathematical Physics ​

Mathematical physics is a branch of applied mathematics that deals with the mathematical modelling of physical phenomena. It involves the study of differential equations, partial differential equations, variational principles, and mathematical methods in physics

  • Theoretical physics

Mathematical Biology ​

Mathematical biology is a branch of applied mathematics that deals with the mathematical modelling of biological systems. It involves the study of population dynamics, epidemiology, ecology, genetics, and evolutionary biology

Mathematical Chemistry ​

Mathematical chemistry is a branch of applied mathematics that deals with the mathematical modelling of chemical systems. It involves the study of molecular structure, chemical reactions, quantum chemistry, and statistical mechanics

Engineering ​

Engineering is a field that applies mathematical and scientific principles to design, develop, and maintain systems, structures, and processes. It involves the study of mechanics, thermodynamics, materials science, and electrical circuits

Numerical Analysis ​

Numerical analysis is a branch of applied mathematics that deals with the development and analysis of numerical algorithms for solving mathematical problems. It involves the study of approximation, interpolation, integration, differentiation, and optimization

Game Theory ​

Game theory is a branch of applied mathematics that deals with the study of strategic interactions between rational decision-makers. It involves the study of games, strategies, payoffs, equilibria, and incentives

Statistics ​

Statistics is a branch of applied mathematics that deals with the collection, analysis, interpretation, and presentation of data. It involves the study of probability, sampling, hypothesis testing, regression analysis, and data visualization

Probability ​

Probability is a branch of mathematics that deals with the study of random events and uncertainty. It involves the study of probability spaces, random variables, probability distributions, and stochastic processes

Optimization ​

Optimization is a branch of applied mathematics that deals with the study of maximizing or minimizing a function subject to constraints. It involves the study of linear programming, nonlinear programming, convex optimization, and combinatorial optimization

Computer Science ​

Computer science is a field that deals with the study of algorithms, data structures, programming languages, and computer systems. It involves the application of mathematical and computational techniques to solve problems in computing

  • Machine learning

Cryptography ​

Cryptography is a branch of applied mathematics that deals with the study of secure communication and information protection. It involves the study of encryption, decryption, digital signatures, and cryptographic protocols
